Sasha Costanza-Chock, Ph.D., speaking via Skype at "The Mobile Migration Workshop" on April 1, 2011 in Nashville at the John Seigenthaler Center.  
  
Costanza-Chock is a Postdoc at the [Annenberg School for Communication & Journalism at USC](https://annenberg.usc.edu/) and a [Fellow at the Berkman Center for Internet & Society at Harvard University](https://cyber.law.harvard.edu/).  He is a cofounder of [VozMob.net (Mobile Voices / Voces M�viles)](https://vozmob.net/en/about), a community media platform for immigrant and low wage workers in Los Angeles to use mobile phones for popular communication and digital storytelling. Next year, he will be joining the faculty of[ MIT's Comparative Media Studies program](https://cms.mit.edu/).  
  
The workshop was co-sponsored by the Online News Association and the Freedom Forum Diversity Institute.  
  
More [videos of the presentations](https://www.youtube.com/view_play_list?p=213C2614F1D17290) at the workshop are on YouTube.
