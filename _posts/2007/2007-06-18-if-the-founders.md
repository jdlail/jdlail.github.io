I often wonder what creativity and daring the entrepreneurial newspaper barons of the early 20th century would have brought to the industry's current situation. Had they lived in this era, they could very well have built empires in completely different markets. But had they chose newspapers, what ideas would have have brought to bear? They were highly individualistic -- more often than not eccentric -- original thinkers and bare-knuckled competitors who took risks.
